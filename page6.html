Entry #0520

I have not seen Marden since that night. The Guild claimed he fell ill. His name was struck from the rosters. His quarters were emptied before I could retrieve the pages I know he kept. But it doesn’t matter. I have found what he found.

The ritual was not designed to burn the ward. It was meant to open something. A channel. A gate. The circle they used was old, scratched into the stone beneath a false floor. I memorized every line. Every symbol. Then I spent a season deciphering them.

Some nights I forgot to sleep. Most nights I forgot to eat. I could feel the meaning behind the markings like a breath against glass. Always there, but never sharp. And then, one evening, with a fresh-cut circle and lines drawn in blood and ash, I spoke the words I had translated.

The room did not shake. There was no thunder or crackling flame. Just stillness. And then a voice, not heard with ears, but known. Like a presence leaning into the spine.

It asked me who I was.

I told it. Nothing more. And it replied:

That is not yet true. But it will be.

I asked it what it was.

It gave no name. Only silence. But in that silence, I understood. It was not offering me a pact. It was not demanding my soul. It had no use for obedience. It had waited, and now it had been found. Or perhaps I had.

I asked it why me. It said only this:

Because you saw what others missed. You watched. You learned. You wanted to know.

And then it was gone. Not banished. Not dismissed. Gone, as if it had never been. But I can still feel where it was. In the corners of the room. Beneath the ink in my journal. It is not a possession. It is a direction.

The circle remains. I have not cleaned it.

— 127 BDP
