Entry #0520

I have not seen Marden since that night. The Guild claimed he fell ill. His name was struck from the rosters. His quarters were emptied before I could retrieve the pages I know he kept. But it doesn’t matter. I have found what he found.

The ritual was not designed to burn the ward. It was meant to open something. A channel. A gate. The circle they used was old, scratched into the stone beneath a false floor. I memorized every line. Every symbol. Then I spent a season deciphering them. It took months to gather what I needed.

Not rare reagents, not power. Understanding.

I mapped the patterns from Marden’s ash-ring to the spiral of the soldier’s wound, to symbols scrawled on the underside of a discarded ledger I found buried behind a false shelf in the guild archives. They aligned. Not perfectly, but precisely enough to complete the equation.

The ritual was not elegant. It was a scaffolding. Broken pieces stitched into function. But it held.

I cleared the storeroom below my quarters. The walls were stone, the air dry. I traced the ring in ink, not blood. I did not yet trust my own body in the equation.

I lit the candles. Burned the parchment. Spoke the words.

At first, silence.

Then the ink began to move. Not run. Move. As though something beneath the surface was breathing through it.

The room darkened unnaturally, like smoke had thickened the shadows. And then, a voice.

Not loud. Not even audible. But I felt it inside the base of my skull, like thought reversed.

"You are not like the others," it said.

I asked what it was.

It laughed. Not cruelly. Patiently.

"I am what you seek."

I demanded knowledge. It offered a name. Not its own, but mine, twisted and ancient.

I refused it.

"Good," it said.

And then the light returned, all at once, and I was alone again. But the ink on the floor still pulsed. And my hand, the one I used to draw the circle, trembled, not with fear, but with something closer to hunger.

I had spoken to a demon. It knew me. And I had not been consumed.

That was the moment I knew:

The line between man and monster is not drawn by nature.

It is drawn by choice.
